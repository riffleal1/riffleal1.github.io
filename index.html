Botany and 3D Design

Using Multi-Dimensional Space to Teach Plant Science

The biology of plants is comprised of a complex network of systems, processes, and interacting parts. Many of these parts are microscopic and difficult, or even impossible to see with the human eye. Botany textbooks often teach these microscopic concepts using separated diagrams and text for each process but donâ€™t generally include a central diagram that displays how all of the processes work together.

Because of this, it can be difficult for students to grasp an idea of how plants work overall. This project will explore the use of multi-dimensional space in teaching major botanical functions, and creates the foundation for an interactive tool that will create a more interactive and realistic learning environment for botany students.
